Five Years of Change:
Tracking Cardiovascular Health Across Australia

To investigate the prevalence of Heart, Vascular, and Stroke Disease in Australia, we will first explore state-level data from 2011 to 2017 to understand how these conditions are distributed across the population. In tandem, we will examine the number of cases across different age groups (e.g., 18–54, 55–74, 75+) and compare trends across states and genders, before taking a focus on Victoria.

Firstly, the overall trend of such diseases in Australia will be Compared. See the small multiples map below which compares the rate of disease incidents in Australia.

Overall, the incidence rate for persons aged 18 and over has decreased from 2011 to 2017. To observe the overall trend from 2011 to 2017, refer to the slope graph below, which also highlights changes in cases by gender.

This slope graph shows the change in incidence rates for persons aged 18+ from 2011 to 2017. Each line represents a state, highlighting which regions saw the greatest improvement or increase in cardiovascular disease prevalence.

Evidently, the disease prevalence in all states has decreased. However, is there a correlation between population number and disease prevalence? See the bubble chart below.

How Does Change of Different Age Groups Change Between 2011 and 2017?

The following charts compare the number of cardiovascular cases across three age groups in Australia for 2011 and 2017, split by gender. This allows us to see which age groups have seen increases or decreases and whether male or female populations are more affected in each period.

Male & Female Cardiovascular Disease Cases Broken Down by Age Group

As seen above, there are a higher number of cases in Males that are aged 75+ and 55-74 in 2013, which is also reflected in 2017. Thus, it can be said that although the total number of disease cases has decreased, Males still have the higher number of disease cases over ages 55.
